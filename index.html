2. The Loadout

Hover the hotspots to see my gear reviews.

Saddle Bag Warning

I strongly advise against 14-18L saddle bags—they bend and break. If you need capacity, go with panniers instead.

Not Recommended

Frame Bag

The Restrap frame bag was okay, but it was always wet in the morning for some reason. Consider waterproof liners.

Mixed

Restrap Handlebar Bag

Great quality and reliable. Perfect for items you need quick access to. Highly recommended for long-distance touring.

Recommended

Hydrapak Seeker 4L

Water bladder that packs down very small when empty. Skip the water filter—you're never more than 30 minutes from a house.

Navigation Device

Get one that clips onto your handlebar. Beats stopping to check Google Maps every 10 minutes. Komoot is great.

Bivy Bag over Tent

Faster, smaller, lighter. Tents take too long. A bivy lets you sleep in riskier spots and leave quickly.

3. The Strategy

How to sleep for free: Garden → Campsite → Wild

1

The Garden Knock

At 5pm every day, start knocking on doors. Houses with a big garden and easy access from public streets.

The Script

{Smile!! Cheerful tone!!!}

"Hello, my name is {firstName}, long story short I've cycled all of the way from {home}, and I'm cycling to {destination}. I have very little money, so was wondering if there was any possibility you'd let me set up my tent in your garden.

{Pause}

Just one night. {Pause} It's a very small tent. {Pause} I'll be away early in the morning."

2

The Campsite

Same script, but ask to "stay here, one night, for free." Spend an hour on this before Plan C.

3

Wild Camping

Find the perfect spot, wait until sunset. Lay out your bivy and sleep under the stars.

4. The Grind

It's a difficult adjustment. You're going from modern day living to caveman living.

There is so much pain. When your sleeping bag is wet. When you are tired. When you are hungry. When you are nervous to knock on doors.

"But every so often you stop, get off of your bike and take in the view. At that moment, it all becomes worth it."

There's nothing like cycling 150 km in a day and then crashing into a vineyard in Italy.

There is so much pain, but there is just as much joy. They balance one another out.

5. The Stories

The magic happens when you take chances on strangers.

Germany

The Farmhouse

Rejection after rejection. Nobody was letting me camp in their garden. In desperation, I found a camping site 45 minutes away. It was getting dark.

While cycling there, I see some people standing in the driveway of a farmhouse. I do my thing and ask if I can camp on their land. They speak in a South German accent.

They bring the mother out, and then the father. Eventually one of them says:

"Yes, or you can stay in the guest house."

It was amazing. I went from living like a caveman to living in a guest hut. It had heating, wifi, lights—everything one could ask for.

The guest hut — from caveman to comfort

They invited me for dinner. There were lots of flies, 3 blind cats, and they all drank from the same water bottle. Above all, they acted like a family. They were close. It was beautiful to see.

Getting rejected from the many people beforehand was the best thing that could have happened to me.
